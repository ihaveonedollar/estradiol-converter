Why do different laboratories report estradiol in different units?

Different laboratories and countries use different measurement systems.

Some report estradiol in pg/mL (picograms per millilitre), while others use pmol/L (picomoles per litre).

The result represents the same hormone concentration—only the unit of measurement is different, much like reporting temperature in Celsius or Fahrenheit.

This converter allows you to accurately convert between the two units when comparing blood tests, reading medical literature, or discussing results with healthcare professionals.
